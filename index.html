How To Recycle

Step 1

Items accepted include working, broken and non-functional devices:
  • iPhones & Smartphones
  • iPads & tablets
  • Handheld game consoles
  • Basic cell phones
Items not accepted and can be recycled locally:
  • Instructional manuals
  • Car chargers
  • Protective cases


Step 2

  • Deactivate phones prior to packing
  • Ensure batteries are installed in the phones (no loose batteries)
  • Pack devices in a small sturdy box or padded envelope
  • Securely tape package to ensure contents safety.


Step 3

  • For 1-4 phones, print off a self paid shipping label from our website and affix postage to package
  • For 5 or more devices, contact us and we can send you prepaid shippping label!
  • Tape shipping label to small box or padded envelope
  • Drop off package at your nearest USPS location or schedule a pickup


Step 4

Our Team processes the devices to determine they can be repurposed or recycled. Congratulations in doing your part of a Sustainable World!

How To Factory Reset Your iPhone

  • Remove any paired devices, such as an iWatch.
  • Go to Settings and sign out of iCloud.
  • Go back to Settings and tap General > Reset > Erase All Content and Settings.
  • If asked for your device passcode or restrictions passcode, enter it. Then tap Erase.

How To Factory Reset Your Samsung

  • If FRP (Factory Reset Protection) is on, go to Settings > Lock screen > Screen lock type and choose None.
  • If you have a Google account, go to Settings > Accounts and backup > Accounts and tap on Google, then tap Remove account.
  • From Settings > Factory Data Reset > Tap Reset > Delete all.
  • If asked for your device passcode enter it. Then tap Delete all.

How To Factory Reset Your Phone

  • If FRP (Factory Reset Protection) is on, go to Settings > Lock screen > Screen lock type and choose None.
  • From Settings > either Backup and Reset or Privacy > Tap Factory Data Reset > Erase everything.
  • If asked for your device passcode enter it. Then tap Delete all.
